## 4. Agent perspectives

### 4a. Quant Researcher

**On B-096 (reporting dates):**
High-value enrichment. Directors are in a blackout period (typically 30 days before results). Buys *within* that window are either a breach (rare) or mis-classified. Buys *immediately after* results are disclosure-lag-corrected genuine bets. Pre-registering the hypothesis: *directors buying within 5 trading days of a positive results announcement will show higher T+21 CAR than the general T1–T3 cohort.* This is a known academic finding (Lakonishok & Lee, 2001 style). Needs a minimum of 30 post-results firings to be meaningful — instrument first, measure in 6 months. **Status: instrument (B-096) first, defer signal spec.**

**On B-097 (market cap / shares outstanding):**
Pre-registered research item #2 from the quant spec. `value ÷ market_cap_gbp` normalises conviction across the size spectrum — a £50k buy at a £20m micro-cap is 10× more conviction than the same amount at a £200m small-cap. This will likely segment the T3 tier meaningfully. However: with ~150 measurable firings, stratifying by market-cap bucket will give sub-N=30 cells quickly. **Instrument now (B-097), measure later — don't promote to a new signal_id until we have 30+ firings per bucket.**

**On B-098 (absolute performance):**
Useful display column, not a signal input. The distinction matters: CAR tells you whether the director's trade captured *excess* return; absolute return tells you whether you made money in absolute terms. Both are true in a bull market; they diverge in a down market. Show both, clearly labelled.

**On B-099 (B1/B2 in cohort chart):**
B1 and B2 shipped Sprint 13 but neither has enough post-launch firings for meaningful CAR at T+90 yet. **The sparkline in the performance table is right — it shows trajectory even with sparse data. The cohort-chart addition (Level 2 scatter) should be built now but may read "N=3 (insufficient)" for months. Build it, label it appropriately.**

**On B-100 (Paper P&L):**
See full proposal in §4d. The quant note: a paper-trade P&L is not the same as a backtest CAR. The backtest computes CAR at T+1/T+21/T+90 for *all historical firings*. A live paper-trade book tracks *only firings since inception*, with open positions mark-to-market daily. Both are needed. They answer different questions: "did this signal work historically?" vs "is it working right now?". **Keep them separate in the data model.**

**On B-104 (transaction size for all buy signals):**
Correct and overdue. The value-tier filter was originally scoped to T1/T2 as a UI simplification, but it creates a distorted view — a £500k T3 NED cluster buy is invisible in the "by value" sort while a £10k T1 CEO buy appears. Fix this before adding the Paper P&L feature, or the P&L bucketing will inherit the same distortion.

**On B-105 (sector indices):**
Strongly endorse. The FTSE All-Share as a universal benchmark for AIM micro-caps is statistically inappropriate — those stocks have beta >1 and sector/size tilts that FTSE All-Share doesn't capture. FTSE AIM All-Share is the minimum; sector-specific sub-indices (FTNMX sectoral codes, already partially in `tickers_meta.benchmark_symbol`) are better. This changes existing CAR numbers — run a diff report before and after so Rupert can see the magnitude of change.

### 4b. Trader

**On B-096 (reporting dates):**
Blackout periods are real and matter legally. The MAR Article 19 closed period is 30 calendar days before an interim or year-end announcement. A buy inside a closed period should be flagged red on the UI — it's either a regulatory breach or (more likely) a mis-parsed date. Add a `closed_period_flag` field. Post-results buys within 2 weeks are the cleanest informational signal because the director knew the numbers and bought anyway once they could.

**On B-097 (market cap):**
Also critical for the cost model. The current 50bps spread assumption is fiction for sub-£100m stocks. Real spread on a £30m AIM name can be 200–300bps. If we're going to show paper P&L (B-100), the cost model must use market-cap-adjusted spread assumptions, not flat 50bps.

**On B-098 (absolute performance):**
Good. Show it alongside CAR, clearly distinguish the two. Label the column "Stock return since tx" vs "vs benchmark". This also surfaces the practical reality that directors are often buying into underperformers (value-style), so absolute performance may be negative even when CAR is positive.

**On B-100 (Paper P&L):**
See full proposal in §4d. The trader's additions: (1) position size should be conviction-weighted — a £1k flat paper trade flattens signal. If the director bought £200k, the paper trade should reflect that scale (or a capped version). (2) exit should default to T+21 (one trading month) since that's the most consistently observable horizon we have — but make it switchable per the B-072 horizon toggle. (3) show gross vs net (after stamp + spread) P&L separately. (4) if a stock is acquired or delisted during the hold window, cap the return at the acquisition premium / last traded price — don't null it.

**On B-104 (transaction size for all buy signals):**
Agree completely. There is no good reason to exclude T3/T4/S1/F1/B1 from a value sort. A T4 (catch-all) director buying £1m of stock is more interesting than a T1 CEO buying £15k for a share plan. Fix the predicate.

**On B-105 (benchmark indices):**
Critical. An AIM stock benchmarked against the FTSE All-Share will look like it has alpha in a risk-on rally simply because AIM outperforms in those regimes. We need FTSE AIM All-Share (`^AIM` or `^FTAS AIM` — check Yahoo Finance ticker). FTSE 100 members against `^FTSE`, FTSE 250 against `^FTMC`. The logic for assigning `benchmark_symbol` from `is_aim` and market cap already exists in `tickers_meta` — this is mostly a price-backfill and a small assignment-logic update. **Run the CAR diff report before and after; expect AIM small-cap CARs to change materially.**

### 4d. Paper P&L solution — design proposal (B-100)

**What it is:**
A live paper trading book that opens a notional position every time a tracked signal fires, tracks it mark-to-market, and closes it at the configured horizon (default T+21). Distinct from the backtest (which covers historical firings) — the paper book covers only firings since the book opened.

**Data model:**
Extend `paper_trades` table (already exists per spec 07) with:
- `opened_at` — date of signal fire
- `entry_price` — next-day open after signal fire (T+1 open)
- `notional_gbp` — position size (options below)
- `signal_id` + `fingerprint` → FK to `signals`
- `closed_at` — T+21 / T+90 (configurable)
- `exit_price` — closing price at `closed_at`
- `gross_pnl_pct`, `net_pnl_pct` (after spread + stamp)
- `status` — OPEN / CLOSED / TAKEOVER / DELISTED

**Position sizing options (Rupert decides):**
1. **Flat £1k per signal** — easiest, ignores conviction. Good for pure signal comparison.
2. **Scale to director's £ conviction** — if director bought £50k, paper trade is £50k. Pros: realistic. Cons: concentrates in large buys.
3. **Capped conviction scaling** — min(director's £ value, £10k) per signal. Recommended: balances realism with diversification.
4. **Risk-parity** — size inversely to recent volatility. Complex; revisit after simple version lives for 3 months.

**Recommended implementation (phased):**
- **Phase A (read-only, no writes):** Display panel on Performance page showing all open paper positions with current MTM P&L. Entry prices from the existing price table; MTM from latest close. No new writes — pure query. Rupert can see it works before committing to the full write path.
- **Phase B (write path, Sprint 28 or later):** `eval_signals.py` opens a paper_trade row when a signal fires; nightly price refresh updates MTM; a separate `close_paper_trades.py` script closes positions at T+21 / T+90.

**Dashboard placement:**
New section on Performance page: "Live paper book" — open positions table (signal, entry date, entry price, current price, MTM P&L %, hold days remaining) + a rolling closed-trades summary (hit rate, avg net P&L at T+21).

**Relationship to backtest:**
Backtest: "if you had followed every historical signal, what would the aggregate CAR have been?"
Paper book: "for signals fired since [date], what is the running P&L today?"
Both are needed; they answer different questions. Never conflate them.
